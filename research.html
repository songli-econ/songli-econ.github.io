  • Robust Narrative Persuasion
    Working paper.

    Abstract: This paper studies a sender who designs a public menu of narratives for a heterogeneous population whose cross-sectional distribution of priors is known only through moment restrictions. After observing a public event, each receiver selects the narrative that best fits his prior and updates within it. We show that under binary states, one threshold-setting narrative per realization suffices. Implementability is bounded by a persuasion frontier, along which there is a trade-off across signal realizations. In a political-narrative application, the dictator's optimal value can fall as the average prior rises: a higher average prior can let Nature meet the variance lower bound with less mass strictly above the persuasion threshold.
